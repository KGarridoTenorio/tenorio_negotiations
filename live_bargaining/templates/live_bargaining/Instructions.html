Overview:

In this exercise, you will participate in multiple rounds of negotiations over a single item:
A 10kg bag of wood pellets.

  • This item is produced by the Supplying company at different quality levels.
  • The Buying and Supplying company need to reach a deal in terms of Price & Quality
  • The Buying company will then sell that item in the market to consumers at a profit.

Note: A higher quality level agreed upon during the negotiation has consequences:

  • For suppliers: higher quality is more costly to produce (PC).
  • For buyers: higher quality will be sold for a higher market selling price to consumers (MP).

    • Both the buying and supplying companies employ AI Bots and humans to negotiate with the representatives of the counterpart.

    For the rest of the experiment, you will play the role of a {% player.role %}.

    Objective:

    Your goal is to agree on a combination of Price and Quality that maximizes your profits.

    Terms of Negotiation:
    • Quality (Q) Ranges from 0 to 4 without decimals.
    • Negotiated Price (NP) Denoted in euros (€) without decimals.
    Roles and Profit Calculation:

    Production Cost (PC) increases by 1€ for each unit increase in Quality.
    The Market Selling Price to the consumer (MP) increases by 1€ for each unit of Quality. {% include_sibling 'table_formulas.html' %} Example for the Supplier: If the base production cost is 1€ at a quality of 0, and you just agreed with the buyer on a negotiated price of 7€ and a quality of 2, then the Supplier profit = 7 - 2 - 1 = 4€
    Example for the Buyer: If the base market selling price to consumer is 10€ at a quality of 0, and you just agreed with the supplier on a negotiated price of 7€ and a quality of 2, then your profit = 10 + 2 - 7 = 5€

    Additional Instructions:
    • You will be randomly rematched each round with a different counterpart.
    • The base values of (MP) for buyers and (PC) for Suppliers are unknown to the counterpart and will vary randomly each round within the following ranges:
      • Base MP could be 8, 9, or 10
      • Base PC could be 1, 2, or 3
